Monadnock Valley Press: Edward de Vere

The Monadnock Valley Press has republished a number of poems by Edward de Vere (1550-1604), the 17th Earl of Oxford:

  • "A Crown of Bays"
  • "Even as the Wax Doth Melt"
  • "Fain Would I Sing But Fury Makes Me Fret"
  • "Framed in the Front of Forlorn Hope"
  • "I Am Not as I Seem to Be"
  • "If Care or Skill Could Conquer Vain Desire"
  • "If Women Could Be Fair and Yet Not Fond"
  • "The Lively Lark Stretched Forth Her Wing"
  • "The Labouring Man That Tills the Fertile Soil"
  • "My Meaning is to Work What Wonders Love Hath Wrought"
  • "My Mind to Me a Kingdom Is"
  • "Sitting Alone Upon My Thought"
  • "The Trickling Tears That Fall Along My Cheeks"
  • "Were I a King"
  • "What Cunning Can Express"
  • "When Wert Thou Born, Desire?"
  • "Whereas the heart at tennis plays"
  • "Who Taught Thee First to Sigh?"
  • "Winged With Desire"
