Monadnock Valley Press: Michel de Montaigne

The Monadnock Valley Press has republished several of the essays by Michel de Montaigne. For the pleasure and edification of the reader, we have provided two translations: that by John Florio published 1603 and read by Shakespeare soon thereafter, and that by Charles Cotton published in 1877 in the edition by William Carew Hazlitt.

  • Of the Education of Children (Florio and Cotton)
  • Of Friendship (Florio and Cotton)
  • Of Idleness (Florio and Cotton)
  • Of Liars (Florio and Cotton)
  • That the Intention Is Judge of Our Actions (Florio and Cotton)
  • That Men Are Not to Judge of Our Happiness till after Death (Florio and Cotton)
  • That the Soul Expends its Passions upon False Objects, Where the True Are Wanting (Florio and Cotton)
